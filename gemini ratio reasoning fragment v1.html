Ratio Drill

The length of a pendulum ($L$) is multiplied by 4. What happens to the Period ($T$)?

Relationship: $T \propto \sqrt{L}$

Factor change in $T$:

Four Ways to Solve:

1. Scaling (Factor Method)

Replace $L$ with the factor 4:
$T_{factor} = \sqrt{4} = 2$.
The period doubles.

2. Divide Equations

$\frac{T_2}{T_1} = \frac{2\pi\sqrt{L_2/g}}{2\pi\sqrt{L_1/g}} = \sqrt{\frac{4L_1}{L_1}} = \sqrt{4} = 2$.

3. Reasoning vs Constant

Because $T$ depends on the square root of $L$, any change in $L$ is "dampened" by the root. To double the period, you need 4x the length.

4. Calculate the Constant (k)

Assume $L=1, T=1$.
$1 = k\sqrt{1} \rightarrow k=1$.
New $L=4$, so $T = 1\sqrt{4} = 2$.
